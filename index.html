About

About Me

I am currently an Assistant Professor at the Indian Institute of Information Technology, Dharwad. My research journey began with a focus on distributed systems and has evolved to encompass blockchain technology, machine learning, and lightweight cryptography for secure, scalable IoT environments.

Prior to joining IIIT Dharwad, I completed my postdoctoral research at the LRC Lab, University of Montreal, where I worked on advancing next-generation security solutions for the Internet of Things. During my Ph.D. at IIT Patna, I contributed to novel architectures for secure cross-chain communication, decentralized access control, and privacy-preserving ownership transfer.

Beyond research, I am passionate about mentoring students, fostering interdisciplinary collaboration, and exploring creative pursuits such as poetry and sustainable art using upcycled materials.

Research

Research Focus

My research lies at the intersection of trust, privacy, and decentralization in complex distributed systems. I focus on designing architectures that enable secure, scalable, and interoperable blockchain ecosystems for real-world applications.

  • Self-sovereign identity frameworks using blockchain and decentralized identifiers (DIDs)
  • Behavior-aware machine learning for federated IoT security
  • Lightweight privacy-preserving techniques: homomorphic encryption, zero-knowledge proofs
  • Scalable consensus mechanisms and interoperable blockchain architectures
  • Real-time threat detection using graph-based and temporal modeling
  • Blockchain-based smart contract verification and enforcement

Current Research Projects

Decentralized Identity for IoT Devices

Building self-sovereign identity (SSI) solutions using DIDs and verifiable credentials to enable secure, privacy-preserving authentication for millions of IoT devices.

Behavior-Aware Federated Learning

Recruiting

Prospective Students

I am looking for motivated PhD students!

Applications are open for Fall 2026 intake at IIIT Dharwad. Research areas include blockchain, federated learning, and IoT security.

What I'm Looking For

  • Strong background in computer science, mathematics, or related fields
  • Genuine curiosity about security, privacy, and decentralized systems
  • Solid programming skills (Python, C/C++, Java, or Solidity)
  • Familiarity with at least one of: blockchain, machine learning, IoT, cryptography
  • Self-motivated, independent researcher with strong communication skills
  • Qualified in GATE / UGC NET (preferred but not mandatory for all positions)

What I Offer

  • Fully funded PhD position with competitive monthly stipend
  • Access to IIIT Dharwad's research infrastructure and compute resources
  • Mentorship for top-tier publications (IEEE, ACM, Springer)
  • Conference travel funding for accepted papers
  • Collaborative environment with peers in blockchain, ML, and security
  • Opportunities for industry internships and international collaborations

How to Apply

Please email me at [email protected] with the subject line "Prospective PhD Student — [Your Name]", and include:

  • Your CV / résumé (with academic transcripts)
  • A short statement of research interests (½–1 page)
  • Your GATE / UGC NET score (if applicable)
  • Names and contact of 2 references (optional but helpful)
  • Links to any publications, code repositories, or projects (if any)

Note: I read every email personally but receive many. Please make your email concise and your CV well-organized. I typically respond within 1–2 weeks.

For Fall 2026 intake, applications through the IIIT Dharwad admission portal typically close in April–May 2026. Early applications are strongly encouraged. Please check the official portal for exact dates and eligibility criteria.
